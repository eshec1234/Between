# Anti-Hallow Market Positioning

**Owner:** Brand & Marketing
**Status:** Active — core counter-positioning

---

## Counter-Positioning Statement

> "We are the anti-Hallow."

---

## What We Are Not

| Feature | Hallow / Spirit-Tech Norm | Between |
|---|---|---|
| User accounts | Required | None |
| Push notifications | Core engagement loop | Never |
| Streaks / badges | Standard gamification | Prohibited |
| Personalized feed | Algorithmic, retention-optimized | Anonymous, location-based only |
| Ads | Standard | Partner custodians only (see bus-operations/partner-custodians.md) |
| Social sharing | Encouraged | Not built |

---

## Differentiation Pillars

- **No profiles:** No user accounts, no gamification of spiritual practice. You come, you feel, you leave.
- **No push notifications:** Not optimizing your spiritual engagement for retention metrics. The place calls you; we don't.
- **No commodified intimacy:** Anonymous by design — not as a feature but as a theological commitment.
- **No streaks/badges:** No gamification of the sacred. Consistency is between you and the place.

---

## Target Persona: Refugee from Spirit Tech

**Who they are:** User who left Headspace, Calm, or Hallow because they felt surveilled, gamified, or that their inner life had been productized.

**What they experience on Between:** Anonymity as relief. No history. No performance. No metrics on their spiritual state.

**Why they matter:** Likely early adopter and strong word-of-mouth source. They've already developed a critique; Between validates it architecturally.
