# Discovery Framing — Beta Launch Copy Guidelines

**Owner:** Brand & Marketing
**Status:** Active — applies to all beta outreach and in-app copy

---

## Core Frame

> "You're among the first to encounter this."

Not: "Try our app." Not: "Download now." Not: "Join thousands of users."

The discovery frame positions Between as a finding — something the user has stumbled upon — rather than a product they are being sold.

---

## Why This Matters

Between's value proposition is uncanny encounter. A traditional app-launch frame ("we just launched!", "check us out!") undermines this by making the experience feel commercial before it begins.

Discovery framing creates the atmosphere of the app before the app opens.

---

## Beta Copy Principles

1. **Avoid launch language.** No "we launched", "now available", "download today."
2. **Avoid urgency mechanics.** No "limited time", "be first", "don't miss."
3. **Speak to felt experience.** Lead with what they might feel, not what the app does.
4. **Prefer the passive voice for the app.** "Places have been gathered" not "we built a database."
5. **Let discovery feel accidental.** Word-of-mouth is the intended distribution channel.

---

## Approved Beta Copy Templates

**Text message / DM share:**
> "I found something. It's called Between. You don't need to believe in anything."

**Email subject lines:**
> "Something I wanted you to find"
> "A place I think you'd feel something in"
> "Not sure how to explain this one"

**In-app first-open copy (see Onboarding.jsx):**
> "You don't need to believe in anything. You just need to have felt something."

---

## Press / Journalist Framing

Do not pitch as a tech product. Pitch as a cultural observation:

> "Thirty percent of Americans identify as religiously unaffiliated — the fastest-growing spiritual demographic. They're having profound experiences in specific places and have nowhere to report them. Between is where those reports go."
