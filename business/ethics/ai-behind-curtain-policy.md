# AI-Behind-the-Curtain Policy

**Effective:** Week 5
**Owner:** Ethics Division
**Status:** Red Line (Non-negotiable)

---

## Policy

AI is used ONLY for internal/operational tasks within Between:

- Flagging incomplete place submissions
- Spotting duplicate entries in the database
- Moderation triage assistance (surfacing reports that need human review)
- Internal summarization for operational teams

---

## Theological Rationale

Between's promise is: *"This is where humans felt something."*

AI-authored place descriptions would structurally hollow out this promise. The app reports human testimony, not synthetic generation. The moment a model writes what someone "might feel" in a place, Between becomes fiction with a map pin.

---

## Prohibition

Under no circumstances shall AI generate:

- Place descriptions
- Experience reports (formerly "reviews")
- User-facing theological or spiritual content
- Category tags that appear to reflect human experience
- Marketing copy that makes metaphysical claims on behalf of a place

---

## Disclosure

If AI-assisted moderation flags content that is subsequently acted upon, internal logs must note this. No disclosure to end users is required for triage-only use (consistent with standard content moderation practice), but the Ethics division maintains the right to audit all AI touchpoints.

---

## Enforcement

Any violation of this policy constitutes a **Tier 0 (critical) ethical breach** requiring:

1. Immediate removal of AI-generated content
2. Review by Ethics division
3. Documentation in the risk register (see bus-operations/risk-register.md)
4. Public disclosure policy TBD case-by-case
