# Indigenous & Tribal Land Exclusion Policy

**Status:** Red Line (Non-negotiable)
**Owner:** Ethics Division
**Review cycle:** Before each 50-entry database milestone

---

## Default Policy

Exclude Indigenous and tribal land sites from the Between database unless **explicit community consent or active partnership** exists with the relevant tribal authority.

---

## Rationale

One listing of a tribal site without consent can generate permanent press coverage framing Between as extractive — regardless of intent. This policy must exist before the database reaches 50 entries, because the window for a clean public record closes fast.

Beyond reputational risk: these sites are often living cultural, spiritual, and sovereign spaces. Mapping them for non-Indigenous users without consent is extraction regardless of how it's framed.

---

## Process

1. **Research phase:** Before adding any place, check Native Land Digital (native-land.ca) for territorial boundaries.
2. **Flag:** If Indigenous or tribal association is identified, the entry is held pending verification.
3. **Verification:** Require written consent from the relevant tribal authority or cultural steward.
4. **Default:** When in doubt, exclude. The burden of proof is on inclusion, not exclusion.
